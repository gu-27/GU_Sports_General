Where we go from here
Two ideas that could
define the next chapter.
Proposed resource
Mental Fitness Lab
A dedicated space where every Gonzaga student can develop the mental skills that sport demands — and life requires.
  • Visualization, mindfulness, and focus as daily practice — not crisis intervention
  • Peer-led cohorts rooted in Ignatian reflection and the daily examen
  • Open to every student — no prior athletic background required
  • Connects the Mind and Spirituality pillars of the formation framework into a single place
  • Distinct from counseling services — this is performance and formation, not treatment
Proposed program · Modeled on Hogan Entrepreneurship
Sports Identity Cohort
A selective, cross-disciplinary cohort for students who want to go deep — applying their major to sports as a career path and formation journey.
  • Competitive application — open to any school: SBA, Engineering, Education, Health Sciences, Law
  • Cohort moves together across two years — peer accountability, shared identity, deep formation
  • Experiential learning at the core: real consulting engagements, practicum placements, applied research
  • Faculty from multiple schools co-facilitate — sports becomes the integrating theme across disciplines
  • Like Hogan for entrepreneurship: a community signal that Gonzaga takes this seriously

"These are not additions to what Gonzaga already does. They are the institutional structures that make sports identity a first-class part of what it means to be a Zag."

It's already happening
GSCG is the model
for what this initiative can be.

The Gonzaga Student Consulting Group shows what happens when students are given a vehicle to apply their academic discipline to the thing that drives them — sports. The result is formation that goes far beyond any single career path.

The engagement
Fall 2025 — 5 students, 10 weeks, one real client: Gonzaga NIL.
Full NIL partnership strategy — partner scoring system, ROI evidence, and 10+ campaign concepts — delivered to a live university client with real stakes.
The sports psychology element
Students didn't just apply business skills — they applied the mindset of sport.
Performance under pressure, iteration through failure, cohort accountability, and the drive to produce work that actually matters — these are the habits sport builds and consulting demands.
Who it prepares students for
Both sports and non-sports careers.
The skills developed — strategic analysis, stakeholder communication, data-driven decision-making — are exactly what every employer wants, whether the industry is sports, healthcare, technology, or finance.
What GSCG proves

"GSCG is not an outlier. It is the template. The initiative gives every school at Gonzaga the same on-ramp — and every student the same chance to grow through sports, no matter where they end up."
